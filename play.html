🌍 Heart of the Congo

An Interactive Journey into Darkness and Discovery

Ready to Begin?

In the autumn of 1890, the Company makes you an extraordinary offer. The pay is enormous, the prospects uncertain. You will venture into the heart of Africa, where civilization fades into shadow and every choice carries the weight of moral consequence.

Heart of the Congo is an interactive narrative experience inspired by Joseph Conrad's timeless novella. Your decisions will shape the story, determining whether you emerge enlightened, corrupted, or lost in the darkness.

This story explores themes of imperialism, moral ambiguity, power, and the darkness within civilization itself. It is intended for mature audiences.

What Awaits You

5 Story Phases

Follow your journey from Brussels to the depths of the African interior, each phase presenting new moral challenges and dangerous choices.

10+ Crucial Choices

Every decision matters. Your choices determine not just what happens, but what kind of person you become through the story.

Multiple Endings

Will you be a witness to truth? A disillusioned wanderer? Complicit in evil? Lost in the wilderness? Each path leads to a different destiny.

No Right Answers

There are no "good" or "bad" choices here—only consequences. Every path reveals different truths about power, morality, and human nature.

The Story Unfolds in Five Acts

I. The Contract: In Brussels, you meet the Company and face your first moral test. Will you resist their authority or accept their doctrine?

II. The Coast: You arrive at a desolate Station. Rumors of a Grove of Death and a mysterious figure named Kurtz await. Will you seek truth or remain willfully ignorant?

III. The River: You journey upstream toward the Inner Station. The jungle presses in. Violence erupts. Will you continue or turn back?

IV. The Inner Station: At last, you meet Kurtz—a man who has become something more than human, or perhaps less. Will you save him or abandon him?

Coda: The Return: The river carries you home. But you are changed. What truth will you carry back to civilization?
